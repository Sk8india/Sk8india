    • All Skateboard Categories

    Street Skateboarding: 40-50%

  • Focuses on tricks performed on urban obstacles like stairs, rails, and ledges.
  • Boards are typically 7.5" to 8.5" wide with hard wheels for better control on smooth surfaces.

    • Park Skateboarding: 20-30%

  • A blend of street and vert, performed in skateparks with a mix of obstacles.
  • Boards and wheels are similar to those used in street skateboarding but can vary depending on the skater's preference.

    • Longboarding: 10%

  • Emphasizes downhill racing, cruising, and carving.
  • Boards are longer (usually 33" to 60"), with larger and softer wheels for better stability and grip at high speeds.

    • Vert Skateboarding:5-10%

  • Involves riding on large ramps, halfpipes, and bowls.
  • Boards are wider, around 8.5" to 9" or more, for better stability at high speeds and on vertical surfaces.

    • Freestyle Skateboarding:5-10%

  • Focuses on technical flat-ground tricks and creative expression.
  • Boards are typically similar in size to street boards but can have a variety of shapes.

    • Downhill Skateboarding:5-10%

  • Concentrates on high-speed descents down steep roads.
  • Long, stable boards with precision trucks and high-grip wheels are used.

    • Cruiser Skateboards:less than 5%

  • Designed for casual riding and commuting.
  • Features a wider deck and softer wheels for a smooth ride over rough terrain.

    • Slalom Skateboarding:less than 5%

  • Involves weaving between cones at high speed.
  • Boards are shorter and narrower, with flexible decks and responsive trucks for quick turns.

    • Freeride Skateboarding:less than 5%

  • A hybrid of downhill and freestyle, focusing on slides and technical maneuvers on slopes.
  • Boards are versatile, often symmetrical, and equipped with medium-hard wheels.

    • Old School Skateboarding:less than 5%

  • Features riding styles and board designs reminiscent of the 1970s and 1980s.
  • Boards are wider with a distinctive fishtail shape and often include larger, softer wheels.
